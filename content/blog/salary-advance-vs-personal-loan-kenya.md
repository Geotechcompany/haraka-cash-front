Both products pay to M-Pesa. Both show fees before you accept. The difference is what you are borrowing against and how you think about repayment.

## Salary advance

A salary advance bridges you to pay day. You already earned the income; you are accessing it early. Repayment is tied to when salary hits your M-Pesa, usually within one month.

Use it when:

- Rent is due three days before salary
- School payment cannot wait
- You overspent on transport but payday is fixed

## Personal loan

A personal loan covers a need that is not tied to your next payslip. Stock for a shop, a medical bill, or a home repair fit here. You still repay in one month on HarakaCash, but the purpose is broader than payroll timing.

Use it when:

- Income is mixed (shop plus side gigs)
- The expense is not synced to salary date
- You need slightly higher first-time limits for a specific purchase

## Fees work the same way

HarakaCash lists processing fee, interest, and principal on the offer for both products. Pick the product that matches your story in the application. Wrong product choice does not change the math much, but honest answers speed approval.

## Which limit applies?

First-time borrowers often start around KES 20,000. Limits climb with on-time repayment and referral activity. Salary earners with stable employers sometimes qualify faster for advances because income is easy to verify.

## Make the call

If pay day is the anchor, choose [salary advance](/apply?product=salary-advance). If the expense stands on its own, choose [personal loan](/apply). Either way, read the offer, pay the processing fee only when you agree, and plan repayment before you tap accept.
