Inventory loans work when stock turns fast enough to repay inside one month.

## Know your sell-through

If 80% of units sell within fourteen days, a one-month loan fits. Slow movers need cash sales, not credit.

## Negotiate supplier terms first

Some wholesalers give seven-day credit to regular buyers. Use digital loans when supplier terms run out, not by default.

## Size the loan to one purchase

Borrow the invoice amount plus small buffer, not "as much as approved." Headroom is for emergencies later.

## Keep supplier receipt

Matches your application story if support asks. Good records speed reviews.

## Repay from margin, not float

Separate shop M-Pesa if you can. Pay loan from marked profit pool after weekend sales.

## Seasonal spikes

Christmas, back-to-school, and Ramadan patterns are predictable. Plan stock six weeks early when possible, borrow two weeks early when not.

## When not to finance stock

Experimental products, expired-near goods with unclear demand, or stock you buy because supplier pressured you.

Finance the shelf that already sells. [Apply for a personal loan](/apply) sized to your next invoice.
