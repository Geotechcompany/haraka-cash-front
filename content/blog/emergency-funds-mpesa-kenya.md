Emergencies do not wait for pay day. An M-Pesa buffer reduces panic borrowing.

## Start with one week of expenses

If monthly costs are KES 40,000, aim for KES 10,000 in a separate M-Pesa line or sacco account before you chase bigger goals. Something beats zero.

## Name the account

Use a second SIM or locked savings wallet labeled "emergencies only." Psychological labels work.

## When to tap the buffer

Medical copay, sudden travel for family, broken phone you need for work. Fashion sales are not emergencies.

## When a loan still makes sense

Buffer empty after two shocks in one month? A one-month salary advance with known fees can bridge to payroll without selling assets. Compare the offer total to what you gain by solving the problem now.

## Replenish after you use it

Treat repayment to yourself like a lender: schedule three small transfers on pay days until the buffer returns.

## Insurance where possible

NHIF, device insurance, and funeral covers remove some spikes entirely. Premiums are boring until you need them.

## Do not park in risky schemes

Emergency cash should not sit in unregulated ventures promising daily returns. Liquidity matters more than yield.

If your buffer is thin today, [check a salary advance offer](/apply?product=salary-advance) and borrow the minimum that solves the problem.
