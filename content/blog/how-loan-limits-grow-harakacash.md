Limits start conservative. They climb when you prove you repay.

## Starting point

Many first loans cap around KES 20,000. That protects new borrowers and the lender while trust is built.

## On-time repayment is the main lever

Settled loans without late days push your ceiling up faster than borrowing the max every time. Consistency beats size.

## Referral bumps

Invite up to ten friends who register with your link. Each adds KES 1,000 of available credit when qualified. Details are in the referrals screen.

## Income updates matter

Promotion or side business growth? Update employment and income fields in profile before the next application. Old data undersells you.

## What slows growth

Late payments, disputed fraud flags, mismatched identity data, and applying for more than you can repay.

## Upper range

Established borrowers with clean history can move toward KES 250,000 over time. Not everyone needs that much. Borrow to the task.

## Limits are not targets

Available credit is a cap, not a goal. Using less keeps repayment light and leaves headroom for real emergencies.
