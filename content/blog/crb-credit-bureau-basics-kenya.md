CRB is the Credit Reference Bureau. Lenders share loan performance data. Other lenders read that history when you apply.

## What appears on your file

Typical entries include:

- Active loan balances
- Days past due
- Settled loans
- Defaults and write-offs

Mobile lenders report monthly. A two-week late payment on another app can show up quickly.

## Thin files are common

Never borrowed before? Your file may be thin. That is not bad. HarakaCash still reviews affordability from your application even when bureau history is short.

## Fix errors before applying

Wrong ID linkage happens. Visit a licensed CRB agent or use official dispute channels if a loan you never took appears. Keep reference numbers.

## Declined after CRB pull

Ask yourself:

- Is any balance overdue right now?
- Did I apply to many lenders this week?
- Did I mismatch ID or phone details?

Clean those up, wait a few days, then try again with accurate data.

## CRB happens after you accept

On HarakaCash, bureau review runs after you accept the offer and pay the processing fee. You already know the repayment amount before that step.

## Good behavior compounds

Six months of on-time repayments across products improves how underwriters see you. One late mark does not end borrowing forever, but it raises costs next time.
