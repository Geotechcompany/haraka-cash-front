Late fees are avoidable for most borrowers who treat due date like rent day.

## Know the exact fee rule

Read [terms](/terms) for how late fees calculate. Fixed amount or percent changes your planning.

## Pay early when possible

No rule says wait until the last hour. Paying five days early costs nothing extra on most one-month products.

## Salary lag plan

If employer pays late sometimes, build a KES buffer equal to one loan payment in a separate wallet. That is your insurance, not another loan.

## Talk before the date

Support can note payroll delay if you call early. Calling after late fee posts is harder.

## Do not borrow to repay without math

Rolling into a new loan to cover an old one without new income is a spiral. Reduce expense or seek payroll advance from employer first.

## Watch M-Pesa float limits

Sometimes you have money but hit transaction caps. Withdraw or split payments per lender instructions before cutoff time.

## One late mark hurts twice

You pay the fee and you pay higher risk pricing next time. The second cost is larger over a year.

Stay fee-free on your current loan. Set a reminder now if you already borrowed, or [plan before you apply](/apply).
