You need cash on M-Pesa today, not next week. That is why digital lenders grew so fast in Kenya. The process is shorter than a bank branch visit, but the steps still matter if you want a clean repayment record.

## Check the offer before you pay anything

A serious lender shows principal, interest, processing fee, and due date on one screen. Read that offer twice. If a number only appears after you pay a fee, stop.

On HarakaCash you see the full breakdown before you accept. The processing fee is paid to move your application into CRB review and payout. Principal and interest stay on the offer you already approved.

## Have your details ready

Most apps ask for:

- Kenyan mobile number linked to M-Pesa
- National ID number
- Employer or income source
- Monthly income and main expenses

Salary earners get faster decisions when pay day and employer name match what you submit. Self-employed borrowers should use realistic income figures. Inflated numbers lead to declines or limits you cannot repay.

## CRB checks are normal

Licensed digital lenders pull your credit bureau file after you accept an offer and pay the processing fee. A thin file is not automatic rejection. Late loans on other apps are a bigger problem.

If you were declined, wait until open balances are cleared before reapplying everywhere. Multiple applications in one week look risky to scoring systems.

## Payout lands on M-Pesa

After CRB clearance, funds go to the M-Pesa number on your profile. Confirm it is yours and active. A wrong number delays payout and creates support tickets you do not need.

## Repay on the due date

One-month products need one clear plan: set a calendar reminder three days before due date and keep the repayment amount in M-Pesa or your bank line. On-time repayment is how your limit grows.
