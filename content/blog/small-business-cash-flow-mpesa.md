Shop owners live in the gap between stock payment and sales. M-Pesa credit can cover that gap if you treat it as working capital, not income.

## Good uses

- Restocking fast-moving SKU before a weekend rush
- Paying supplier on delivery when float is thin
- Covering rent when sales were strong but cash is still in Till float

## Risky uses

- Betting on a product you never sold before
- Paying personal bills unrelated to the shop
- Rolling one loan into another without sales to support it

## Match term to turnover

HarakaCash one-month term fits inventory that sells within weeks. If stock sits ninety days, a shorter bridge loan still needs a clear exit from margin.

## Track unit margin

Before you borrow KES 15,000 for stock, know expected gross profit. Repayment should come from that profit pool, not tomorrow's opening float.

## Separate SIMs mentally

Many owners use one M-Pesa for shop and home. Tag mentally what is business cash. Mixing makes you think you can repay from money already spent on household items.

## Build supplier trust

On-time stock payments earn credit from wholesalers. Digital loans are a backup when that trust line is not enough.

## Repay from peak days

If Saturday is your best sales day, schedule internal reminders for Sunday morning transfer to loan repayment.
